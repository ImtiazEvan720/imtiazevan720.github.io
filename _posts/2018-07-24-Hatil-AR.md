HATIL AR is an Augmented Reality Application for shoppers around the globe who want to place and experience HATIL furniture at their own space.

![Hatil_Ar](/assets/img/hatil_ar.jpeg)

**My Role :** _Lead Developer_ 

**Tech Stack :** _Unity3d, ARKit, ARCore, AWS S3_ 

**Platform :** _iOS, android_

**Project Description :**

HATIL AR was an **augmented reality** application designed to let shoppers preview HATIL furniture in their own spaces before making a purchase. Users could browse the product catalog, select furniture items, and place accurately scaled 3D models in their real environment through AR, creating a more realistic and confident shopping experience.

My contribution focused on developing the **Unity3D** application from the ground up, excluding the creation of the 3D furniture assets themselves. A major challenge was keeping the mobile app lightweight, scalable, and efficient for both iOS and Android users, since the full 3D furniture catalog was too large to ship directly with the application binary. To solve this, we structured the app so that only a small set of base assets shipped with the initial install, while additional furniture models were downloaded on demand.

The 3D furniture assets were packaged as Unity AssetBundles and hosted on **AWS S3**. The mobile app downloaded assets only when users needed them, reducing the initial app size and improving the installation experience. Product metadata, catalog information, and update availability were managed through backend **REST API** services, allowing the app to detect newly available furniture assets and present them to users for optional download.

I also worked on integrating **in-app payment** workflows while maintaining compliance with Google **Play Store** and Apple **App Store** policies. Alongside feature development, I focused on keeping the codebase modular and maintainable by applying software design patterns and writing testable components so the project could be extended later and handed over smoothly to other development teams.

Overall, the project combined Unity3D, AR, AWS S3 asset delivery, REST API integration, mobile deployment, in-app payment support, and maintainable application architecture to create a scalable AR shopping experience for HATIL customers.
